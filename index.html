Health & Safety Meeting Guide

Our monthly meeting, made easy.

Hey team, I cleaned up the Google Sheet and put this guide together to make the monthly meeting easy. Follow it top to bottom; every link and code you need is one tap away.

- Kevin

Use the arrow keys or the buttons below to move through the five steps.

Press → or click Next to begin

STEP 1 / 5 · Set up the meeting

Attendance, date & quorum

Open the cleaned-up committee Google Sheet. Take attendance, set today's date, and confirm we have quorum before any decisions get made.

  1. Open the committee Google Sheet (button below).
  2. Mark who's present and update the meeting date.
  3. Confirm quorum is met. The sheet tells you on the right-hand side.
Open the Google Sheet ↗
Quorum check

The sheet calculates quorum for you on the right-hand side. If it doesn't show quorum met, note it and hold any votes until enough members are present.

STEP 2 / 5 · Record attendance in OATS

Log monthly SM attendance

Switch your system to OATS and submit this month's Safety Member attendance.

  1. Go to go/srm.
  2. Change your system to OATS.
  3. Open the Reports tab.
  4. Click Monthly SM Attendance.
  5. Paste the codes below, then hit Submit.
Attendance codes for OATS
923438,49889,876218,840404,870681,896777,853360,849386,856382
STEP 3 / 5 · Inspections

Monthly committee inspections

Walk through this month's committee inspections together and keep the list current.

  1. Review the monthly committee inspections.
  2. Update the inspection list. Add what's new, close what's done.
  3. Flag anything that needs follow-up so it carries into business.
Keep it current

An up-to-date inspection list is the first thing a JHSC review looks at. Update it live during the meeting, not after.

STEP 4 / 5 · Records & business

HOIRs, SROs & open business

Check our HOIRs and SROs, update the records, and discuss. Then move each item through new, held, and completed business.

  1. Review open HOIRs and SROs; update records and discuss each.
  2. Update new, held, and completed business.
  3. Carry anything unresolved into held business so it isn't lost.
Where to find them

HOIRs: search your email for incident# to pull up the one you need.

SROs: they live on the FIT Jira dashboard. Open the SRO dashboard ↗

Held business, for example

Say there's an SRO for a pole that needs removing. We already know about it, but it hasn't been done yet. It stays in held business until it's complete.

STEP 5 / 5 · Open floor

Anything else for the committee

Close the meeting with an open round before you adjourn.

  1. Raise anything else worth bringing to the committee's attention.
  2. Share good reminders for our coworkers: seasonal hazards, PPE, near-miss reporting.
  3. Confirm action items and owners before you adjourn.
Before you close

Make sure every action item has a name and a date next to it. Without that, it slips to next month.

That's the whole meeting.
